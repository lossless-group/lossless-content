# Harvard Graduate School of Education
![Exterior view of Harvard Graduate School of Education’s Gutman Library on Appian Way in Cambridge, Massachusetts](https://www.gse.harvard.edu/sites/default/files/styles/16x9__focal_point_scale_and_crop__md/public/2025-08/gutman-2025.jpg?h=ca0eecd2&itok=Oykbvuxh)

_The Harvard Graduate School of Education is Harvard University’s education-focused graduate school, combining research, professional preparation, and innovation to shape leaders and ideas in learning worldwide. [^pq0g8v]_

It is a **graduate school of education within Harvard University** that offers master’s, doctoral, and professional education programs focused on improving teaching, learning, and educational systems. [^pq0g8v] It traces its origins to Harvard’s early 20th‑century teacher education efforts and is located in Cambridge, Massachusetts, on Appian Way, adjacent to Harvard’s main campus. [^pq0g8v] From a consultant’s perspective, it is tracked as a globally influential hub for education policy, leadership development, and evidence-based practice that shapes how education systems evolve.

# What They Do

HGSE’s day-to-day work centers on **graduate degree programs, research, and professional education** that support teachers, school and system leaders, policymakers, learning designers, and other education professionals. [^m7b3ce] [^pq0g8v] It generates value by conducting research on learning and schooling, preparing leaders for roles across education sectors, and delivering executive education and certificate programs for practitioners globally. [^m7b3ce] [^pq0g8v]

- **Graduate degree programs** in education (master’s and doctoral) that prepare students for roles in teaching, leadership, policy, research, and learning design. [^pq0g8v]

# History and Origin Story

HGSE emerged as Harvard University’s specialized graduate school dedicated to education, formalizing Harvard’s earlier teacher education and educational research efforts into a distinct school located on Appian Way in Cambridge, MA. [^pq0g8v] Over time it has evolved from a focus on training teachers to a broader mandate that includes policy, leadership, learning design, and global education innovation, with growing emphasis on equity and evidence-based practice. [^pq0g8v]

- **Early 20th century (contextual)** – Harvard concentrates its education-related efforts, laying groundwork for a dedicated graduate school of education associated with the Cambridge campus on Appian Way. [^pq0g8v]
- **Development of professional and executive education (by the 2000s–2010s)** – HGSE expands into short, non-degree programs for practitioners and leaders, later branded as offerings like HGSE’s Unbound professional learning. [^m7b3ce]
- **2023 (metadata era)** – HGSE describes its mission as preparing “education leaders and innovators” to expand opportunities and outcomes for learners, signaling an explicit innovation and impact framing.
- **May 13, 2026** – HGSE announces that **Russell Booker** is appointed as a Joint Senior Fellow at HGSE Initiatives, with a term from July 2026 to June 2028, reflecting ongoing investment in practitioner-scholars and cross-initiative leadership. [^nyw4oy]
