# Cedars-Sinai Medical Center

![Exterior view of Cedars-Sinai Medical Center hospital campus in Los Angeles](https://www.cedars-sinai.org/content/dam/cedars-sinai/social-media/images/cedars-sinai-fb.jpg)

_*Cedars-Sinai Medical Center is a major academic medical center and nonprofit health system in Los Angeles that combines high-volume clinical care with research and medical education at scale.*_

Cedars-Sinai Medical Center is a nonprofit academic medical center and part of Cedars-Sinai Health System, providing hospital care, specialty care, and primary care services across the Los Angeles region. [^b80lt1] [^yb40tf] It operates a large tertiary and quaternary care hospital in Los Angeles and an affiliated network of physicians and outpatient facilities under entities such as Cedars-Sinai Medical Group and Cedars-Sinai Medical Care Foundation. [^yb40tf] [^457hfd] Cedars-Sinai traces its roots to Jewish community hospitals founded in Los Angeles in the early 1900s that later merged to form the current medical center. [^7ayojy] Consultants track it as a bellwether for cutting-edge clinical programs, patient volume, and innovation in U.S. hospital and health-system strategy. [^yb40tf] [^457hfd] [^7ayojy]

## Mission and Identity

> “Cedars-Sinai is a nonprofit, independent health care organization committed to providing world-class care to patients from around the world.”[^7ayojy]

Cedars-Sinai describes itself as “a leader in providing high-quality healthcare encompassing primary care, specialized medicine and research” serving patients locally and internationally. [^7ayojy] It emphasizes “compassionate care,” “pioneering research,” and “education” of healthcare professionals as central pillars of its role as an academic medical center. [^7ayojy] The organization positions itself as both a community anchor for Los Angeles and a destination center for complex care. [^7ayojy]

- **Stated values / principles:** Cedars-Sinai highlights commitments to quality, innovation in research, education of physicians and other health professionals, and serving the diverse communities of Los Angeles while attracting patients “from around the world.”[^7ayojy]

## What They Do

Cedars-Sinai Medical Center delivers inpatient and outpatient clinical care, including primary care, specialty care, and advanced tertiary and quaternary services (such as cardiology and transplantation) through its hospital and affiliated physician groups. [^yb40tf] [^457hfd] [^7ayojy] It operates as an academic medical center that trains residents and fellows and conducts biomedical and clinical research, integrating care delivery with teaching and scientific discovery. [^mzfxt2] [^7ayojy]

- Comprehensive inpatient hospital services at Cedars-Sinai Medical Center in Los Angeles, covering a wide range of medical and surgical specialties. [^7ayojy]
- Outpatient primary care and internal medicine practices, including Cedars-Sinai Medical Group and Cedars-Sinai Medical Care Foundation primary care clinics in locations such as Marina del Rey and Playa Vista. [^b80lt1] [^yb40tf] [^457hfd]
- Specialty cardiovascular care through cardiology and interventional cardiology programs, reflected in dedicated providers like interventional cardiologists and heart specialists. [^mzfxt2] [^7ayojy]
- Multi-specialty physician group practice under Cedars-Sinai Medical Care Foundation, which offers specialties including addiction medicine, advanced heart failure and transplant cardiology, allergy/immunology, anesthesiology, cardiac electrophysiology, and many others. [^457hfd]
- Graduate medical education and fellowship programs in internal medicine, cardiology, and other specialties, where physicians complete residency and fellowship training at Cedars-Sinai. [^mzfxt2] [^7ayojy]
- Clinical research and trials embedded within specialty programs, particularly in cardiovascular medicine and other high-acuity fields. [^mzfxt2] [^7ayojy]
- Community health and outreach services, as part of its nonprofit mission to serve the diverse communities of Los Angeles. [^7ayojy]

## History and Origin Story

Cedars-Sinai Medical Center evolved from early 20th‑century Los Angeles Jewish community hospitals that merged over time into a single major nonprofit academic medical center serving the broader region. [^7ayojy] The modern Cedars-Sinai Medical Center grew through expansion of its hospital campus, development of multi-specialty physician groups, and the creation of a formal health system structure, while building strong programs in cardiovascular care, research, and education. [^yb40tf] [^mzfxt2] [^457hfd] [^7ayojy]

- **1900s (early):** Jewish community members in Los Angeles established separate hospitals that would later become the predecessors of Cedars-Sinai. [^7ayojy]
- **1960s–1970s:** Community hospitals merged and expanded, ultimately consolidating into Cedars-Sinai Medical Center as a major nonprofit hospital in Los Angeles. [^7ayojy]
- **Late 20th century:** Cedars-Sinai formalized its role as an academic medical center, expanding residency and fellowship programs and investing in research infrastructure. [^mzfxt2] [^7ayojy]
- **2000s:** Development and growth of Cedars-Sinai Medical Group and Cedars-Sinai Medical Care Foundation strengthened its physician network and outpatient presence. [^yb40tf] [^457hfd] [^7ayojy]
- **2010s–2020s:** Continued expansion of regional clinics (e.g., Playa Vista, Marina del Rey), and strengthening of signature institutes like the Smidt Heart Institute and Barbra Streisand Women’s Heart Center. [^b80lt1] [^yb40tf] [^mzfxt2] [^7ayojy]
