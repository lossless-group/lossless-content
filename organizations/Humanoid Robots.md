Several companies are leading the development of humanoid robots, supported by notable investors and funding. Here's a summary of key players, their funding history, and progress:

### **1. [[organizations/Agility Robotics|Agility Robotics]]**
- **Founded:** 2015  
- **Robots:** Cassie (2017), Digit (2020) for logistics and warehouse tasks.  
- **Funding:** $28.8M from investors like Playground Global and ITIC. [^6rqw07] [^9t39dr]  
- **Progress:** Partnered with Amazon to test Digit in logistics operations. [^9t39dr]

### **2. [[organizations/Apptronik|Apptronik]]**
- **Founded:** 2016  
- **Robots:** Apollo for industrial and commercial applications.  
- **Funding:** $350M Series A in 2025, backed by Google and others. [^bd3lf6] [^0famkp]  
- **Progress:** Collaborated with NASA and NVIDIA; aims to price robots below cars. [^0famkp]

### **3. [[organizations/Figure AI]]**
- **Founded:** 2022  
- **Robots:** Figure 01 (logistics) and Figure 02 (2024).  
- **Funding:** $70M Series A (2023) led by Parkway VC; $675M Series B (2024) at a $2.6B valuation from Microsoft, NVIDIA, Amazon, and OpenAI. [^hgx6jt] [^55m6e3]  
- **Progress:** Partnerships with BMW, OpenAI, and Microsoft for AI integration. [^55m6e3]

### **4. UBTECH Robotics**
- **Founded:** 2012  
- **Robots:** WalkerX, Cruzr, AimBot for home, retail, healthcare, and industrial use.  
- **Funding:** $940M over four rounds. [^6rqw07] [^9t39dr]  
- **Progress:** Recognized as a leader in consumer robotics with global reach. [^6rqw07]

### **5. [[Persona AI]]**
- **Founded:** 2024  
- **Robots:** Early-stage humanoids for manufacturing tasks.  
- **Funding:** $10M pre-seed from U.S., London, and Singapore investors. [^0e146h]  

### Other Notable Players:
- **[[organizations/Boston Dynamics|Boston Dynamics]]**: Known for Atlas humanoid robot focused on agility and mobility. [^9t39dr]  
- **PAL Robotics**: Offers customizable humanoid robots for logistics and research applications. [^s30rm5]  
- **1X**: Raised $98M in 2024 to develop humanoids like NEO for hazardous tasks. [^032vuu]

These companies are advancing humanoid robotics with significant investments targeting logistics, manufacturing, healthcare, and consumer applications.
