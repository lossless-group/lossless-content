# Value Proposition & Features

Dograh is an **open‑source, self‑hostable voice agent platform** that lets teams build production‑grade AI voice agents while retaining full control over models, telephony, workflows, and data. [^6vdk6j] [^dlt1s0] [^dg6ok5] It is positioned as a **developer‑friendly, self‑hosted alternative to Vapi, Retell, Bland, and similar voice AI platforms**, deployable on users’ own infrastructure or on Dograh’s cloud. [^6vdk6j] [^dlt1s0] [^dg6ok5]

Dograh provides a drag‑and‑drop workflow builder for **speech‑to‑text (STT), text‑to‑speech (TTS), telephony, and real‑time audio pipelines**, enabling custom call centers, assistants, and other voice applications. [^6vdk6j] [^dg6ok5] It supports integration with multiple AI models (including Dograh’s own hosted models via service keys) across cloud, self‑hosted, and local instances, so a single billing account and key can power voice agents in any deployment mode. [^6vdk6j] [^7zkorf] [^dlt1s0] The platform is explicitly aimed at organizations needing **data sovereignty and compliance**, such as regulated industries, while still offering a managed cloud option. [^6vdk6j]

**Key features (priority order)**  
- **Open‑source, [[Vocabulary/Self-Hosting|Self-Hostable]] voice AI platform** for creating custom voice agents and pipelines. [^6vdk6j] [^dlt1s0] [^dg6ok5]  
- **Drag‑and‑drop workflow builder** for STT, TTS, telephony, and agent logic, designed for production voice agents. [^6vdk6j] [^dg6ok5]  
- **Flexible model integration**, including Dograh’s own AI models consumed via service keys that work across cloud, self‑hosted, and local instances. [^7zkorf] [^dlt1s0]  
- **Telephony integrations** (e.g., Vobiz/Vobuz), with configuration for numbers, caller IDs, and inbound workflows for phone‑based voice agents. [^4x1emy] [^dlt1s0]  
- **Real‑time audio processing** for use cases like AI call centers, real‑time translation, and interactive voice assistants. [^6vdk6j]  
- **Self‑hosted alternative to Vapi & Retell**, emphasizing control over models, telephony, workflows, and integrations for developers. [^dlt1s0] [^dg6ok5]  
- **Cloud app (app.dograh.com)** for teams that prefer a managed deployment but still want the same workflow and model capabilities. [^7zkorf] [^dg6ok5]  
- **Focus on data sovereignty and compliance**, targeting organizations in regulated sectors that must keep voice data under their control. [^6vdk6j]

# Competitive Landscape

## Who it's for, who it's not for

Dograh is for **developers, technical teams, and companies** that want to build **production voice agents** with fine‑grained control over models, telephony, workflows, and deployment, especially those in regulated industries requiring self‑hosting and data sovereignty. [^6vdk6j] [^dlt1s0] [^dg6ok5] It also targets businesses that want a **white‑label voice agents platform** similar to [[Tooling/AI-Toolkit/Agentic AI/Vapi|Vapi]] or [[Bolna]] but prefer an open‑source stack and the option to host on their own infrastructure. [^dg6ok5] [^m0m8p3]

Dograh is less suitable for **non‑technical users seeking fully no‑code, turnkey solutions** with minimal configuration, or organizations that do not want to manage any infrastructure and prefer purely managed, opinionated SaaS with limited customization. [^6vdk6j] [^dlt1s0] It is also not ideal for teams whose requirements are basic and can be met by generic contact‑center or IVR tools without the need for custom models, workflows, or self‑hosting. [^6vdk6j] [^dlt1s0]

## Viable Alternatives

- **Vapi** – A managed voice AI platform for building phone and browser‑based voice agents; Dograh explicitly positions itself as an open‑source, self‑hosted alternative to Vapi. [^dlt1s0] [^dg6ok5]  
- **Retell** – Another voice AI/voice agent platform; Dograh markets itself as “the open‑source, self‑hostable alternative to Vapi & Retell.”[^dg6ok5]  
- **Bland** – Mentioned alongside Vapi and Retell as a platform Dograh is an alternative to, indicating similar voice agent capabilities with a more managed, less self‑hosted focus. [^dlt1s0]  
- **Bolna** – Referenced in promotional content as a platform whose white‑label voice agents offering Dograh can replicate in a self‑hosted, open‑source manner. [^m0m8p3]
