# Advanced Documents Overview

Advanced documents take us beyond flat files and static pages. They combine rich structure, embedded data, interactive views, and programmable behaviors to turn documents into living systems.

This Map of Content collects tools, concepts, and essays about building and working with advanced documents across the Lossless ecosystem.

## Types of Advanced Documents

### Structured Knowledge Docs
Documents with explicit structure: sections, typed blocks, schemas, and relationships between entities.

### Data-Backed Documents
Docs that stay in sync with underlying data sources such as databases, APIs, or spreadsheets.

### Interactive Workspaces
Documents that embed queries, filters, dashboards, and controls so teams can explore and manipulate information in place.

### Workflow-Driven Documents
Docs that orchestrate sequences of steps, approvals, or automations, acting as the visible surface of a deeper process.

## Key Concerns for Advanced Documents

**Schema & Structure**: How content is modeled, typed, and organized.
**Linking & Relationships**: Connections between documents, entities, and external systems.
**Versioning & History**: How changes are tracked, reviewed, and rolled back.
**Collaboration**: Real-time editing, comments, and roles for different contributors.
**Performance & Scale**: Handling large documents, embeds, and queries gracefully.

## Choosing Tools and Frameworks

When evaluating tools for advanced documents, consider:

- **Editing Model**: Block-based, rich text, markdown, or custom schemas
- **Storage Model**: Files, databases, CRDTs, or hybrid approaches
- **Embedding & Integrations**: How easily you can connect data sources, media, and external APIs
- **Programmability**: Support for formulas, scripting, automations, and custom components
- **Export & Interop**: How well content moves between systems and formats

Within the Lossless toolkit, you will find document systems, note-taking tools, and knowledge platforms that support these patterns.

## Patterns & Best Practices

1. **Design the Structure First**: Start from the information architecture and entities you need to represent.
2. **Make Links Cheap**: Encourage dense interlinking between documents, concepts, and tools.
3. **Separate Content from Presentation**: Keep structure and meaning independent from visual styling.
4. **Treat Documents as APIs**: Expose structured data and views that other systems can consume.
5. **Invest in Governance**: Define ownership, review flows, and archival strategies.

## Working with AI in Advanced Documents

AI can augment advanced documents in several ways:

- **Structured Capture**: Help users normalize and structure freeform content.
- **Summarization & Synthesis**: Generate overviews, changelogs, and executive summaries.
- **Navigation & Discovery**: Suggest related documents, entities, and views.
- **Refactoring Content**: Propose reorganizations, extractions, and schema improvements.
- **Interactive Agents**: Allow conversational interaction with the document and its underlying data.
