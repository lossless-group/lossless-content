[^qcfmy8] [^g5haoe] This work is crucial because the protection of biodiversity and ecosystems supports the foundation of human civilization, providing resources, climate stability, and ecosystem services.

![International Union for Conservation of Nature concept diagram or illustration](https://www.tigersafariindia.com/blog/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/iucn-international-union-for-conservation-of-nature.jpg)

Founded in 1948 in Fontainebleau, France, the IUCN unites governments, non-governmental organizations, scientists, and experts from more than 170 countries to collaboratively address global environmental challenges. [^qcfmy8] [^0c70zh] [^g5haoe] Its mission is to "influence, encourage and assist societies throughout the world to conserve nature and to ensure that any use of natural resources is equitable and ecologically sustainable". [^qcfmy8] The IUCN acts as a convener and knowledge hub, offering scientific research, policy recommendations, and technical guidance rather than directly mobilizing public campaigns. [^p4e2n9] 

A practical example of IUCN’s impact is the internationally recognized **IUCN Red List of Threatened Species™**, which provides comprehensive data on the conservation status and extinction risk of species globally. [^qcfmy8] [^p4e2n9] [^g5haoe] This list is a critical tool for governments and conservation organizations in prioritizing and strategizing their conservation efforts. The IUCN also played a foundational role in creating pivotal environmental agreements, including the Ramsar Convention on Wetlands, the World Heritage Convention, the Convention on Biological Diversity (CBD), and the Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species (CITES). [^p4e2n9]

Among its many practical applications, the IUCN’s expertise is used to:
- Advocate for the creation of protected areas and national parks.
- Influence environmental policy at global and national levels.
- Guide sustainable development through publications like the World Conservation Strategy, which helped define and promote the concept of sustainable development globally. [^p4e2n9]
- Provide resources and technical assistance to member states and partners. [^nt8h1d]

The organization’s benefits are numerous, including promoting biodiversity, combating climate change, supporting livelihoods that depend on natural resources, and enhancing scientific collaboration. However, it also faces challenges such as balancing development goals with conservation priorities, securing continuous funding, and navigating political complexities among member countries.
