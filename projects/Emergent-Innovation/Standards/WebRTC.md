WebRTC, or **Web Real-Time Communication**, is an open-source technology standard that enables real-time, peer-to-peer communication directly between web browsers and mobile applications. [^z42mkw] [^925r3k] By providing high-quality audio, video, and data exchanges without requiring plugins or external software, WebRTC has revolutionized how developers build interactive and instant communication services on the web. Its significance lies in empowering seamless collaboration, instant video chats, and live media streaming, all embedded natively within modern browsers. [^9eorgr]

![WebRTC concept diagram or illustration](https://blog.zegocloud.com/wp-content/uploads/2024/05/webrtc.jpg)

At its core, **WebRTC enables direct communication** between two endpoints by leveraging powerful JavaScript APIs within HTML5-enabled browsers. [^5u4gaf] [^9eorgr] Unlike traditional web connections that rely on establishing and maintaining links with a central server, WebRTC enables browsers to securely connect using a suite of protocols—facilitating efficient, low-latency transmission of media and data. [^925r3k] This peer-to-peer approach eliminates common bottlenecks and enhances privacy since sensitive media streams do not traverse intermediary servers. [^4f6t2k]
