# How - Why How We Do Anything Means Everything
[IMAGE 1: Cover of Dov Seidman’s book “HOW: Why HOW We Do Anything Means Everything…in Business (and in Life)”]

_An ambitious business book by ethics-focused entrepreneur **Dov Seidman** that argues competitive advantage now comes less from *what* organizations do and far more from *how* they behave, lead, and govern._

This source is a **business and leadership book** written by Dov Seidman, founder and CEO of compliance and ethics firm LRN and of The HOW Institute for Society.[^4472k1][^r9vgn5][^w8blx5] It was first published in 2007 under the title *How: Why HOW We Do Anything Means Everything…in Business (and in Life)* and later reissued in an expanded edition.[^qinb5k][^bbti1g][^m14427] Innovation consultants return to it because it provides a structured “HOW philosophy” and moral leadership framework for building trust-filled, values-driven cultures in a transparent, interconnected world.[^r9vgn5][^j42l17][^7hbcav][^e1k3jv]

# The People Behind It

- Dov Seidman is the **author** of *HOW: Why HOW We Do Anything Means Everything*, which is described by his official site and The HOW Institute as a **New York Times best seller**.[^r9vgn5][^w8blx5][^7hbcav]
- He is the **founder and CEO of LRN**, a company focused on helping organizations foster principled performance and ethical cultures, and also the **founder and chairman of The HOW Institute for Society**, a nonprofit dedicated to moral leadership and values-based behavior.[^4472k1][^r9vgn5][^w8blx5][^emf7q3]
- Seidman’s academic background includes **bachelor’s and master’s degrees in moral philosophy from UCLA and Oxford, a law degree from Harvard, and an honorary doctorate from Hebrew Union College**, grounding the book in a blend of moral philosophy and legal thinking.[^w8blx5]
- Fortune magazine has called Dov Seidman “the hottest advisor on the corporate virtue circuit,” and he has been named one of the “Top 60 Global thinkers of the Last Decade” by The Economic Times, signaling his influence in corporate ethics and leadership discourse.[^4472k1]
- Seidman’s HOW philosophy and the book grow out of “many of my insights…born out of my training in law and moral philosophy” combined with years advising leaders in boardrooms and shop floors around the world.[^emf7q3]

# Catalog of Notable Works

Since this is a **book**, this section focuses on key chapters and major arguments in *HOW: Why HOW We Do Anything Means Everything…in Business (and in Life).* Exact chapter titles are not fully enumerated in accessible summaries, but its core arguments and themed sections are consistently described across reviews and publisher material.[^qinb5k][^bbti1g][^m14427][^e1k3jv] Where precise chapter titles are unavailable, this list reflects **major thematic arcs** in the book:

- **“Era of Behavior” framing** — The book opens by arguing that we have entered an “Era of Behavior,” where sustainable advantage lies in *how* we behave rather than *what* we do, because “Whats are commodities, easily duplicated or reverse-engineered.”[^qinb5k][^e1k3jv] This sets the central thesis that competitive differentiation now depends on conduct, culture, and values.[^qinb5k][^bbti1g][^e1k3jv]
- **From “What” to “How”** — A recurring argument urges readers and organizations to “move from WHAT to HOW,” being “passionate, inspired, and self-governed by your values and morals,” reframing innovation and competition around behavior rather than products.[^qinb5k][^bbti1g][^e1k3jv]
- **Narratives of behavior and mastery** — Through “short narratives and storytelling techniques,” Seidman examines how the process guiding our thoughts and behaviors is critical to success, arguing that true mastery and innovation require critical thinking, exploration, and experimentation, not just knowing *what* to do.[^bbti1g]
- **Leadership and management elements: trust, reputation, culture, self‑governance** — The book includes chapters that “lead readers through an exploration of common leadership and management elements: communication, competition, transparency, trust, individual/organizational reputation, culture and self-governance,” illustrating how each becomes a source of advantage when approached as a **HOW** issue.[^bbti1g]
- **Case studies and interviews on “hows” of success** — Seidman uses “entertaining anecdotes, surprising case studies, cutting-edge research… and revealing interviews with a diverse group of leaders, business executives, experts, and everyday people” to uncover values-inspired “hows” of twenty-first-century success and significance.[^qinb5k]
- **Moral leadership framework (“HOW philosophy”)** — The book is positioned by The HOW Institute and Seidman’s site as “a philosophical leadership framework for a reshaped world,” offering an ethic of human behavior, a philosophy for individual and organizational behavior, and a values-based approach to leadership.[^r9vgn5][^w8blx5][^j42l17][^7hbcav]
- **Expanded Edition updates** — The expanded edition adds an updated foreword and enhancements reflecting how the Era of Behavior has intensified, with Seidman noting that “competitive advantage has shifted to how we do what we do” and that our behavior now matters in ways he “never imagined” when he first wrote the book.[^m14427][^e1k3jv]

# Why It Matters to Innovators

- **Frames behavior and culture as sources of competitive advantage** — The book argues explicitly that “sustainable advantage and enduring success…now lie in the realm of how, the new frontier of conduct,” making it a foundational text for treating culture, trust, and reputation as designable strategic assets rather than soft side-effects.[^qinb5k][^bbti1g][^e1k3jv]
- **Provides a moral leadership framework for a reshaped world** — HOW is described as “a philosophical leadership framework for a reshaped world” shaped by technological, social, and economic upheaval; innovators can use this to design organizations that are resilient, ethical, and trustworthy in transparent, interdependent markets.[^r9vgn5][^j42l17][^7hbcav]
- **Clarifies how transparency and connectivity reshape organizational risk and opportunity** — Seidman connects social media, technology, and the “disappearance of distance” to new demands on how organizations behave, arguing that these forces give outsiders “MRI vision” into institutions and make HOW issues central to reputation and license to operate.[^j42l17][^e1k3jv]
- **Offers practical lenses on trust, self‑governance, and culture as systems** — By exploring communication, competition, transparency, trust, reputation, culture, and self-governance as recurring motifs, the book gives innovators concrete levers for designing [[concepts/Organizational Culture]] and values-based [[concepts/Governance Risk and Compliance|Governance Risk and Compliance]] systems that enable principled performance.[^bbti1g]
- **Connects behavior to innovation and mastery** — Seidman argues that “true innovation” occurs only when individuals engage deeply with the process of learning, experimentation, and reflection on *how* to most effectively approach tasks, making HOW-thinking a precursor to sustainable [[concepts/Ambidextrous Organizations|Ambidextrous Organizations]].[^bbti1g]
