# Redesigning Work
![Book cover of "Redesigning Work" by Lynda Gratton, showing title and author prominently](https://sloanreview.mit.edu/wp-content/uploads/2022/07/Gratton-4step-redesign-work-fig1.png)

*An evidence-based management book by Lynda Gratton that gives leaders a practical blueprint for how to rethink jobs, teams, and workplaces in the post‑pandemic, hybrid era.*

"Redesigning Work" is a **book** by London Business School professor **Lynda Gratton**, first published in 2022, that tackles how organizations should reconfigure work after COVID-19 and the rise of hybrid arrangements. It builds on Gratton’s long-standing research on the future of work and draws on company case studies to offer a step-by-step process for leaders. Innovation consultants return to it because it combines rigorous people research with a very usable design framework for reshaping work, not just talking about flexibility in the abstract.

# The People Behind It

- **Lynda Gratton** is a professor of management practice at **London Business School**, where she has focused on the future of work, organizational behavior, and human resource strategy.
- She is the founder of the research consultancy **HSM Advisory**, which works with companies on “future of work” and people strategy issues and provides many of the cases and data in the book.
- Gratton has written several influential books on work and organizations, including *The Shift* and *The 100-Year Life*, which similarly explore how demographics, technology, and social change are reshaping work and careers.
- She is recognized globally as a thought leader on work design and has been listed among the top management thinkers by Thinkers50.

# Catalog of Notable Works

Key chapters / arguments in *Redesigning Work* (chapter titles and structure summarized from publisher and catalog descriptions):

- **"Understand"** — Lays out how leaders should rigorously gather data on how work is currently done, what employees value, and how networks and knowledge flows operate before changing anything.
- **"Reimagine"** — Encourages leaders to creatively envision future work arrangements, including hybrid models, by experimenting with time (when work happens) and place (where it happens), grounded in business strategy.
- **"Model and Test"** — Advocates building and simulating alternative work designs, pressure-testing them against core principles such as fairness, productivity, and inclusion before broad rollout. [^pvvf8d]
- **"Act and Create"** — Focuses on implementation: how leaders role‑model new behaviors, co-create with employees, adjust HR systems, and ensure new ways of working become embedded rather than fads. [^pvvf8d]
- **"The Four-Step Process for Redesigning Work"** — An integrative framework Gratton popularized (and later outlined in MIT Sloan Management Review) that runs through understanding people and jobs, reimagining work, modeling and testing, and acting so the redesign sticks. [^pvvf8d]
- **Case study chapters (various firms)** — Interspersed throughout the book are case-based chapters showing how specific organizations applied hybrid and flexible work redesigns in different industries, offering concrete examples for practitioners.
- **"The Great Redesign"** — Connects the book’s ideas to the broader shift toward skills-based organizations and new talent models, arguing this redesign transforms how companies plan, deploy, and develop people. [^2poiyc]

# Why It Matters to Innovators

- It provides a **four-step design framework** (Understand → Reimagine → Model & Test → Act & Create) that innovators can treat like a playbook for redesigning jobs, workflows, and collaboration patterns, not just office layouts. [^pvvf8d]
- Gratton insists leaders start with **data on how work really happens**—mapping networks, flows of knowledge, and what people value—before making changes, which aligns with evidence-based [[concepts/Organization Design]] and reduces the risk of shallow “flexibility theater.”[^pvvf8d]
- The book reframes hybrid work as an opportunity to **experiment with time and place as design variables**, enabling new combinations of synchronous/asynchronous and on-site/remote work that can unlock productivity and autonomy. [^pvvf8d]
- It highlights how **skills and capabilities**, rather than fixed roles, should drive work design—connecting closely to skills-based talent models and internal talent marketplaces that support continuous [[concepts/Business Model Innovation]]. [^ktv4qx] [^2poiyc]
- For corporate innovators, it’s a practical guide to **change adoption**: Gratton details how to involve employees in co‑creation, adjust HR systems, and use leadership role‑modeling to ensure new ways of working stick rather than being discarded at the first downturn. [^pvvf8d] [^95btjk]
