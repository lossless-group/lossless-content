# Business Model Navigator

![Book cover of "Business Model Navigator" by Oliver Gassmann, Karolin Frankenberger, and Michaela Csik, featuring a stylized navigator map with 55 business model patterns](https://vebimo.wordpress.com/wp-content/uploads/2020/03/4steps_navigator.png)[^9xvp7n]

*_The Business Model Navigator is a seminal book and methodology from the University of St. Gallen that equips innovators with 55 recombined patterns to systematically ideate and refine profitable business models.*[^ddob2f][^azk0v4][^p9mwyv]

**Format details**
- Publisher: Pearson (German edition via Amazon link), with associated tools like pattern cards and software; first detailed in research papers around 2012–2014, book published circa 2021.[^azk0v4][^09cdus][^9xvp7n][^j0g1ij]
- Length: Core framework identifies 55 business model patterns; expanded into interactive database, cards, and methodology with four dimensions (Who, What, How, Value).[^ddob2f][^azk0v4][^p9mwyv]

**Where it lives:** [Homepage](https://businessmodelnavigator.com)[^p9mwyv] [Shop for cards/books](https://bmi-lab-shop.myshopify.com)[^09cdus] [Google Books](https://books.google.com/books?id=XXXX) (canonical search-derived; no frontmatter URL provided)

# The People Behind It
- **Oliver Gassmann**: Professor at University of St. Gallen, founder of BMI Lab, led over five years of research identifying 55 business model patterns; author of the book and developer of the Navigator methodology.[^azk0v4][^09cdus][^38iktu]
- **Karolin Frankenberger**: Co-author and researcher at University of St. Gallen / BMI Lab, contributed to pattern cards and the recombination-based ideation process.[^azk0v4][^09cdus]
- **Michaela Csik**: Co-developer of the Business Model Innovation Pattern Cards, collaborator on the practical tools extending the book’s framework.[^09cdus]

# Catalog of Notable Works
- **Who dimension**: Focuses on target customers and stakeholders, ensuring alignment with market realities through iterative refinement.[^ddob2f][^azk0v4]
- **What dimension**: Defines the value proposition and offer, emphasizing creativity techniques like recombination of existing patterns.[^ddob2f][^azk0v4]
- **How dimension**: Covers operational processes, pricing, and internal capabilities for consistent execution.[^ddob2f]
- **Value dimension**: Proves profitability by linking all dimensions, with checklists like value network methodology to validate the full model.[^ddob2f][^azk0v4]
- **55 Business Model Patterns**: Core catalog of proven patterns (e.g., classified by pricing, efficiency), designed for mixing into novel models.[^ddob2f][^azk0v4][^38iktu]
- **Iterative Development Loops**: Methodology's emphasis on grinding patterns across dimensions for coherence and profitability.[^ddob2f][^azk0v4]

# Why It Matters to Innovators
- Distills five+ years of research into 55 empirically-derived patterns from successful companies, enabling "outside-the-box" recombination over invention from scratch—proven in practice for business model innovation.[^azk0v4][^38iktu]
- Structures ideation across four interdependent dimensions (Who-What-How-Value), diagnosing inconsistencies in nascent models and installing mental models for profitable fit.[^ddob2f][^azk0v4]
- Counters "sacred cows" with free thinking and tools like pattern cards/software, illuminating efficiency, pricing, and add-on innovations `[[concepts/Business Model Canvas]]`-adjacent but pattern-focused.[^ddob2f][^azk0v4][^09cdus]
- High practical impact via interactive database and cards, transforming theory into deployable frameworks for incumbents and startups alike.[^p9mwyv][^09cdus]
