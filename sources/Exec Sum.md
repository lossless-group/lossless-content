# Exec Sum

![Exec Sum newsletter logo banner from execsum.co showing the brand name and tagline about curating major news from Wall Street to Silicon Valley](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/format=auto,onerror=redirect/uploads/publication/logo/f2aedf85-682c-47c2-8ad0-f44433524834/Screen_Shot_2021-11-12_at_12.05.03_AM.png)

_Media-savvy finance people treat Exec Sum as their fast, meme-laced morning brief on what matters from Wall Street to Silicon Valley, written so busy dealmakers will actually read it._[2][3]

Exec Sum is a **daily business and markets email newsletter** that summarizes “major news from Wall Street to Silicon Valley, with a touch of memes.”[3] It is produced under the Litquidity media umbrella and promoted as “the daily newsletter that curates major news from Wall Street to Silicon Valley,” with hundreds of thousands of subscribers in the investment banking, hedge fund, VC, and founder communities.[2][3][6] Litquidity began promoting Exec Sum publicly by 2021 as part of its expansion from a memes account into a finance-media brand.[2] Consultants come back to it because it filters macro, markets, and tech dealflow into a single, high-signal brief tailored to how finance and startup operators actually talk and think.[2][3]


## Type and Format

- **Type:** This source is a **newsletter-style digital publication** focused on financial markets, macroeconomics, and tech/startup news.[2][3]

- **Format details**
  - Exec Sum is **digital-only**, delivered primarily as an email newsletter hosted on Beehiiv (execsum.co) with a web archive of issues.[1][3][4]
  - It follows a **weekday daily cadence**, with issues that open on markets and macro headlines, then cover corporate news, fundraising, and cultural items, often with sponsor placements.[1][2][4]
  - The newsletter is **free to read**, monetized via sponsorships and ads targeted at financial professionals and founders (e.g., fintechs and SaaS tools advertising in-issue).[1][4][8]
  - It sits within the **Litquidity** media portfolio, which reports “250k subs on Exec Sum” in its 2023 year-end letter, indicating substantial scale among finance readers.[6]

- **Where it lives:**  
  - [Homepage](https://www.execsum.co)[3]  
  - Secondary surface: Litquidity’s site promoting the newsletter: [“May I share Exec Sum with you?”](https://litquidity.co/may-i-share-exec-sum-with-you)[2]


## The People Behind It

- Exec Sum is produced by **Litquidity**, a finance-media brand that began as an anonymous Instagram meme account focused on Wall Street culture and has since expanded into newsletters, podcasts, and brand collaborations.[2][6]
- A Litquidity post pitches Exec Sum with the line: “May I summarize major news from Wall Street to Silicon Valley, with a touch of memes, for you?”, explicitly tying the newsletter’s voice and curation to the Litquidity persona.[2]
- Litquidity’s 2023 year-end letter notes that the brand surpassed “800k followers on Litquidity and 250k subs on Exec Sum,” highlighting that Exec Sum is a core product line in its media portfolio.[6]
- Individual issues use an informal first-person voice and feature sections like sponsor messages, markets, macro, fundraising, and commentary, but do not prominently list a named editor, suggesting the newsletter is authored under the Litquidity brand rather than a front-and-center individual byline.[1][4][5]

## Why It Matters to Innovators

- **Curated cross-over between Wall Street and Silicon Valley.** Exec Sum explicitly positions itself as the daily newsletter that bridges “major news from Wall Street to Silicon Valley,” giving innovators a single stream that tracks capital markets, M&A, macro, and startup fundraising together.[2][3] This is especially relevant for anyone working on [[concepts/Business Model Innovation]] or navigating the finance–tech interface.
- **Real-time signal on capital flows and valuations.** Issues routinely summarize big fundraises, valuations, and asset allocations (e.g., mega-rounds, AI-driven startups, alternative asset managers), giving innovators a running sense of where money is moving and where narratives are overheating.[1][5][8] This helps calibrate expectations around [[concepts/Market Timing]] and investor appetite.
- **Finance-native framing and language.** The voice is tuned to investment bankers, institutional investors, and VCs (“read by 300K+ investment bankers, institutional investors, venture capitalists, founders, and more”), which means strategic themes are framed in terms of multiples, AUM, macro risk, and deal pipelines rather than generic business advice.[3][6] For innovators who must sell into or partner with these stakeholders, this is a direct line into how they think and talk.
- **Pragmatic use of AI and tools in content and research workflows.** In the AI tools issue, Litquidity describes how it uses ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity, Adept, Bigdata.com, and Narakeet to research markets, generate outlines, synthesize filings, and produce content.[3] For innovators working on [[Vocabulary/Workflow Automations|Workflow Automation]] or [[concepts/Explainers for AI/AI-Augmented Knowledge Work]], this provides concrete patterns of AI-tool chains in a high-pressure, information-dense domain.
- **Window into emerging fintech and B2B SaaS targeting sophisticated buyers.** Sponsors in Exec Sum include niche fintech and software products for alternative asset managers, corporate finance teams, and other sophisticated buyers (e.g., tools for vendor management, expense allocation, or market data).[1][4][8] This gives innovators a running catalog of B2B products and go-to-market narratives aimed at high-value finance customers, useful for [[Vocabulary/Go-to-Market|Go-to-Market]] and category mapping.
