# Ideo U
![IDEO U homepage showing online course tiles and “Design Thinking” branding](https://cdn.shopify.com/s/files/1/0256/6193/1601/files/DVFR_Framework.png?v=1755730212)

*_IDEO U is IDEO’s online school for design thinking and creative leadership, turning the firm’s consulting know‑how into structured, practice-based learning for individuals and teams._* [3][4]

IDEO U is an online education platform created by global design and innovation firm IDEO to “equip you with skills, mindsets, and tools to help you stay relevant and adaptive in our modern world.”[3][4] It was launched in the mid‑2010s as a way to teach the methods IDEO has “practiced for decades” in human‑centered design and innovation.[1][3][4] The platform offers cohort-based and self‑paced courses, certificates, and custom programs for organizations, focused on creative problem solving, innovation, and leadership.[1][3][4] Innovation consultants return to IDEO U because it packages IDEO’s field‑tested design thinking practices into accessible formats they can reuse with clients.

## The People Behind It

- IDEO U is “part of IDEO, an award‑winning global design firm that takes a human‑centered, design-based approach to help organizations innovate and grow.”[3][4]
- IDEO itself was founded in 1991 in Palo Alto by David Kelley, Bill Moggridge, and Mike Nuttall as a global design and innovation company focused on human‑centered design.[1][2]
- Suzanne (often referred to as Suzanne Gibbs Howard in external contexts) is described as an entrepreneur, design strategist, and digital learning expert who “founded and served as Dean of IDEO U” while a partner at IDEO, after previously leading IDEO’s Systems and Organizational Change studio and growing its design research and strategy capabilities globally.[5]
- IDEO U courses are taught by IDEO practitioners and leaders such as David Kelley, founder and chairman of IDEO and founder of Stanford’s Hasso Plattner Institute of Design (“the d.school”), whose enduring contributions are in human-centered design methodology and design thinking.[5]

## Why It Matters to Innovators

- IDEO U distills IDEO’s human‑centered design and design thinking practice—developed over “nearly 50 years” of designing products, services, brands, experiences, and systems—into structured learning, making it a direct channel into one of the field’s most influential playbooks.[2][3][4]
- The platform focuses on equipping learners with “skills, mindsets, and tools” rather than just theory, which makes it particularly useful for practitioners who need to apply methods to real projects and to embed innovation mindsets in teams.[3][4]
- By being “part of IDEO,” which is known for helping organizations “build creative cultures, sustain innovation, and launch new ventures to fuel growth,” IDEO U’s content is grounded in live consulting work rather than purely academic frameworks—valuable for applied innovation, corporate venturing, and [[Vocabulary/Design Thinking|Design Thinking]].[2][3]
- IDEO U’s mission to help individuals and organizations become “more resilient, creative, and innovative” aligns closely with challenges around leading change, building experimentation cultures, and managing through uncertainty—core concerns in [[concepts/Innovation Culture]] and [[concepts/Human-Centered Design|Human-Centered Design]].[3][4][7]
- The availability of certificates and company‑wide programs makes IDEO U a practical tool for scaling a common innovation language and toolkit across large organizations, complementing in‑house training and other external programs.[1][3][4]
