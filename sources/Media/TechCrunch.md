# TechCrunch

_TechCrunch is a long-running tech news site that sits at the center of the global startup and venture ecosystem, surfacing early signals on companies, capital, and categories before they hit the mainstream. [^7w4gk8] [^575y1o]_

TechCrunch is a **digital technology news publication** focused on startups, venture capital, and the broader tech industry. [^7w4gk8] [^575y1o] It was founded as a blog in June 2005 by Michael Arrington and Keith Teare through their venture studio Archimedes Ventures, initially profiling emerging Web 2.0 companies. [^7w4gk8] An innovation consultant keeps returning to TechCrunch because it consistently combines early-stage company coverage, funding data (through Crunchbase historically), and industry analysis that reveals where software, consumer, and infrastructure trends are heading. [^7w4gk8] [^1lhex8]

# Type and Format

- **Type:** This source is a **magazine / newspaper / website** (a digital news publication about technology and startups). [^7w4gk8] [^575y1o]

- **Format details**
  - TechCrunch is a **digital-only** media property that publishes news articles, analyses, and opinion pieces online rather than as a print magazine. [^7w4gk8] [^575y1o]
  - It was **founded in June 2005** as a blog focused on Web 2.0 and startup profiles. [^7w4gk8] [^575y1o]
  - TechCrunch was **acquired by AOL in 2010** for around **$25 million**, and is now part of the broader media portfolio that evolved from AOL’s assets (later under Verizon’s media group). [^7w4gk8]
  - The brand is described as “a leading technology media property, dedicated to obsessively profiling startups, reviewing new Internet products, and breaking tech news,” which reflects its ongoing editorial stance and business model as an ad- and sponsorship-supported news site. [^7w4gk8]

- **Where it lives:**  
  [Homepage](https://techcrunch.com)[^dq03k4]

# The People Behind It

- **Founders**
  - **Michael Arrington** is the primary founder; TechCrunch was launched in June 2005 by Arrington and Keith Teare through their venture studio Archimedes Ventures as a blog that profiled and reviewed emerging Web 2.0 companies. [^7w4gk8]  
  - Arrington had previously worked as a **corporate and securities lawyer** at firms including Wilson Sonsini Goodrich & Rosati and built his own startups, including the payments company Achex, which he sold before starting TechCrunch. [^7w4gk8]  
  - **Keith Teare** co-founded TechCrunch with Arrington via Archimedes Ventures, helping position it as an early hub for Silicon Valley startup coverage. [^7w4gk8]

- **Editorial leadership**
  - Over time, TechCrunch has had several editors-in-chief; today it operates as a professional newsroom within a larger corporate media group, with an editor-in-chief role overseeing coverage of startups, venture capital, gadgets, and major tech platforms. [^7w4gk8] (Specific current name can change; consultants should check the masthead directly on TechCrunch’s site for the latest.)

- **Signature writers / contributors**
  - TechCrunch is widely recognized as a “central voice in Silicon Valley” because Arrington’s early “around-the-clock writing and networking from his home in Atherton, California” helped build its reputation among founders and investors. [^7w4gk8]  
  - Over the years, it has built a team of staff reporters and columnists covering beats such as venture capital, fintech, enterprise software, climate tech, and mobility, many of whom publish recurring columns and analysis pieces that shape conversation in those niches. [^7w4gk8] [^dq03k4]

# Catalog of Notable Works

*(As a publication, TechCrunch’s “works” are its recurring sections, columns, and signature content streams rather than a single canonical article.)*

- **Startup and funding coverage**
  - **Startup profiles and launch coverage** — TechCrunch’s foundational product is detailed write-ups on early-stage startups, often among the first mainstream mentions of companies that later become category leaders. [^7w4gk8] [^vb9eov]  
  - **Funding announcements** — For many years, TechCrunch was closely linked with Crunchbase data and routinely broke news on seed, Series A, and growth rounds, making it a go-to source for deal flow visibility. [^1lhex8]

- **Thematic and tactical columns**
  - [TC+ fundraising and growth columns](https://techcrunch.com/2023/11/13/use-linkedin-to-raise-a-series-a/) — written by operators, investors, and advisors, these pieces offer tactical guidance on topics like raising a Series A via LinkedIn, go-to-market, and founder operations, blending practitioner insight with TechCrunch’s startup audience. [^dq03k4]  
  - **Opinion and analysis on Web trends** — From its origins in Web 2.0 to current coverage of AI, crypto, and climate tech, TechCrunch regularly publishes analysis explaining how new technological waves might reshape markets and user behavior. [^7w4gk8] [^vb9eov]

- **Data-driven and platform content**
  - **Crunchbase corpus and data releases** — TechCrunch historically maintained and released the Crunchbase corpus, containing structured data on companies, people, and funding rounds plus associated TechCrunch news articles, which researchers use for startup ecosystem analysis. [^1lhex8]  
  - **Event coverage and liveblogs** — TechCrunch articles frequently cover major tech events (product launches, conferences, regulatory hearings), offering real-time or same-day reporting for practitioners tracking market shifts. [^7w4gk8]

# Why It Matters to Innovators

- TechCrunch acts as an **early-warning radar** for emerging startups and categories, giving innovators a running feed of new entrants, funding signals, and business models—raw material for competitive intelligence and opportunity spotting. [^7w4gk8] [^1lhex8]  
- Its funding and startup coverage helps founders and product leaders benchmark their own **traction, fundraising milestones, and positioning** against peers and adjacent players, informing strategies around [[concepts/Minimum Viable Product]], category design, and timing. [^7w4gk8] [^1lhex8]  
- The TC+ style tactical articles (e.g., on using LinkedIn to raise a Series A) provide **operator-grade playbooks** on growth, fundraising, and investor relations that innovation leaders can adapt to their own corporate venturing and intrapreneurship efforts. [^dq03k4]  
- By tracking waves from Web 2.0 through mobile, cloud, and now AI and climate tech, TechCrunch offers a **longitudinal view of tech disruption**, helping innovators see how infrastructure, consumer behavior, and capital cycles interact over time. [^7w4gk8] [^vb9eov]  
- For corporate innovators and investors, TechCrunch coverage plus Crunchbase data has become part of the **deal sourcing and landscape scanning stack**, complementing internal scouting with a broad, continuously updated view of the global startup ecosystem. [^7w4gk8] [^1lhex8]
