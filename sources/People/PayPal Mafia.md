## The Concept of the PayPal Mafia

The term "PayPal Mafia" was popularized by Fortune in 2007, accompanying a famous photo of PayPal alumni styled as mobsters. [^q824tu] At its core, the phenomenon describes a group of visionaries—Peter Thiel, [[Sources/People/Elon Musk|Elon Musk]], Max Levchin, [[Sources/People/Influencers/Reid Hoffman|Reid Hoffman]], and many more—who initially joined forces to create PayPal, a revolutionary digital payments company that changed how money moves online. [^a1umrh] [^nk92c4] [^v3zz63] The original PayPal arose from the merger of Thiel’s Confinity (founded with Max Levchin and Luke Nosek) and Musk’s [[organizations/X|X]].com in the late 1990s. Their success culminated in eBay acquiring PayPal for $1.5 billion in 2002, after which many early employees left to pursue new ventures. [^a1umrh] [^nk92c4] [^v3zz63]

What sets the PayPal Mafia apart is not just the creation of PayPal itself, but the ripple effect of its alumni. After the acquisition, members went on to create, invest in, or lead numerous companies that have become pillars of the tech world. These achievements demonstrate the practical power of talent density, shared values, and network effects among entrepreneurial teams. [^4xx8f0]
