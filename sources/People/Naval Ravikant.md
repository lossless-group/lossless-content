# Naval Ravikant
![Headshot of Naval Ravikant speaking on stage at a tech conference](https://files.startupranking.com/person/thumb/187_f1996fd36b543e9142af878eeb7002ac57eb9e7e_naval-ravikant_l.png)

*Naval Ravikant is a tech founder–turned–angel investor whose writing and interviews have become a de facto playbook on building wealth, startups, and a calm, rational life philosophy.*

Naval Ravikant is an **Indian-born American entrepreneur, angel investor, and thinker**, best known as co‑founder and former CEO/chairman of AngelList, a platform connecting startups with investors and talent. [^6ev18s] [^j9uld8] He co‑founded Epinions (later part of Shopping.com) and Vast.com, and has personally invested early in companies like Uber and Twitter. [^6ev18s] [^1febgr] [^jvvn8c] Born in 1974 and active in startups since the late 1990s, he has, over the past decade, become widely followed for his essays, tweets, and podcast episodes that blend startup strategy, wealth creation, and philosophy. [^6ev18s] [^bgk70p] [^jvvn8c] Consultants return to Naval as a source because he compresses complex ideas about leverage, technology, and personal agency into clear, quotable mental models that travel well inside organizations. [^bgk70p] [^jvvn8c]

# The People Behind It

Since the “source” is a person, this section is Naval’s own bio and career arc.

- **Origin and education**
  - Naval Ravikant was **born November 5, 1974, in New Delhi, India**, and later immigrated to the United States, becoming an Indian‑born American entrepreneur. [^6ev18s] [^jvvn8c]
  - He **graduated from Dartmouth College** with a degree in Computer Science and Economics. [^jvvn8c]

- **Key entrepreneurial roles**
  - He **co‑founded Epinions in 1999**, a consumer review site that ultimately went public as part of Shopping.com. [^6ev18s] [^1febgr] [^jvvn8c]
  - He later **co‑founded Vast.com**, a white‑label classifieds and vertical search company. [^6ev18s] [^1febgr]
  - In 2010 he **co‑founded AngelList**, serving as CEO and later chairman; AngelList created a widely used platform for startup fundraising and talent matching. [^6ev18s] [^j9uld8] [^1febgr]

- **Investor and public intellectual**
  - Naval is an **active angel investor**, with early investments in Uber, Twitter, Yammer, Postmates, Stack Overflow, and others, often cited as “dozens of companies.”[^6ev18s] [^1febgr] [^jvvn8c]
  - He co‑founded **Venture Hacks**, an online startup advice forum that published term sheet analyses and fundraising guidance for founders. [^j9uld8] [^jvvn8c]
  - He is known for his **podcast and essays** on wealth, decision‑making, philosophy, and happiness, hosted via his site Naval.com and “The Naval Podcast.”[^bgk70p] [^jvvn8c]
  - He has been a **recipient of the Edmund Hillary Fellowship**, recognizing high‑impact entrepreneurs. [^6ev18s]

- **Recent activity**
  - In **2023, Naval co‑founded Airchat**, described as a social media application built around voice notes and AI‑powered transcription. [^6ev18s]
  - He continues to appear on major podcasts and at events as a commentator on startups, crypto, and the future of technology. [^jvvn8c]

# Why It Matters to Innovators

- **Wealth creation as a skillset, not luck.** Naval’s “How to Get Rich (without getting lucky)” framework repositions wealth building as a learnable process based on **specific knowledge, accountability, and leverage**—a lens that helps founders and operators design careers around compounding advantages rather than short‑term wins. [^bgk70p] [^jvvn8c] This maps directly to how innovators think about **career capital** and aligns with ideas like [[concepts/Leverage]] and [[concepts/Founder-Market Fit]].
- **Operational definition of leverage.** He popularizes a crisp taxonomy of leverage—**labor, capital, and products with no marginal cost of replication (code and media)**—which encourages innovators to favor software, automation, and content embedded in their business models over purely hiring more people. [^bgk70p] [^jvvn8c] This strongly connects to [[concepts/Scalability]] and [[concepts/Automation]].
- **Founder–investor alignment and fundraising transparency.** Through Venture Hacks and AngelList, Naval helped normalize transparent terms, safer seed instruments, and syndicated investing, pushing the ecosystem toward more **founder‑friendly fundraising structures**. [^j9uld8] [^1febgr] [^jvvn8c] For innovators, this is a practical playbook for structuring rounds and choosing investors aligned with long‑term value creation, echoing [[concepts/Startup Finance]].
- **Decision‑making and judgment as core edge.** Naval repeatedly emphasizes **judgment and clear thinking** as the highest‑leverage skills, suggesting that good decisions scale across domains and that reading, reflection, and mental models are key investments. [^bgk70p] [^jvvn8c] This dovetails with [[concepts/Mental Models]] and informs how innovation leaders allocate their learning time.
- **Integration of philosophy and performance.** His work connects **stoic and Eastern philosophical ideas** (e.g., detachment, present‑moment awareness) with high‑performance entrepreneurship, offering innovators a framework for **avoiding burnout and status games** while still pursuing ambitious goals. [^6ev18s] [^bgk70p] [^jvvn8c] This links to [[concepts/Founder Resilience]] and [[concepts/Intrinsic Motivation]].
